Security Architecture

Trusted by compliance teams.

Every layer of Argus is designed to meet enterprise security requirements — not retrofitted to them.

🔑
JWT + API Key Authentication

Short-lived access tokens, 30-day refresh rotation, Redis-backed blocklist for instant revocation. API keys are SHA-256 hashed — the raw key is shown exactly once.

🏛️
Google & Microsoft SSO

OAuth2 SSO with Google Workspace and Microsoft Entra ID. Your team logs in with their existing work credentials. PKCE state validation on every flow.

🎭
5-Tier RBAC

Super admin, org admin, analyst, viewer, and API client roles — each with precisely scoped permissions. 14 granular permission types enforced on every API endpoint.

☠️
License Kill Switch

Argus validates licences every 24 hours against a remote server. Instant remote suspension works even on self-hosted deployments. 72-hour grace period for network issues.

📋
Full Audit Logging

Every login, action, configuration change, and report generation is recorded per tenant with IP address and timestamp. Ready for compliance evidence on demand.

🔐
TLS 1.3 Throughout

All traffic encrypted in transit. HSTS enabled with preload. HTTP strictly redirected to HTTPS. Rate limiting at 60 req/min with account lockout after 5 failed logins.

Common questions

FAQ

How long does setup take?

15 minutes. Create an account, enter your domains and IP ranges, set your report recipients, and click Scan Now. Your first threat report is ready before your coffee gets cold.

Do I need to install anything?

Nothing. Argus is entirely cloud-hosted. No agents, no appliances, no VPN. Your team accesses it via browser and receives reports via email, Slack, or your SIEM.

What if we want to host it ourselves?

Enterprise plan clients can deploy Argus on their own Kubernetes or OpenShift infrastructure with full support from Tesseract Security. Your data never leaves your environment.

How is Argus different from a SIEM?

A SIEM processes events from inside your network. Argus watches the world outside — threat feeds, breach databases, dark web, exposed infrastructure. They complement each other; Argus even feeds directly into your SIEM.
